About Us

Welcome to Zoo Park, one of the most loved and respected wildlife parks in the region.


Our zoo was officially established in 1995 with a vision to create a safe and natural home for animals while providing visitors with an opportunity to learn about the beauty of wildlife.


What began as a small community project with a few local species has grown into a world-class zoological park spread over 120 acres of lush green land.


From the beginning, our goal has been simple — to protect endangered species, promote conservation, and inspire a love for animals in people of all ages.


Over the years, the zoo has evolved into a home for more than 1,500 animals, representing over 180 species from across the globe.


You can find majestic lions, playful monkeys, graceful giraffes, colorful birds, powerful elephants, and fascinating reptiles — all living in carefully designed natural habitats.


The founders, Mr. Gold Smith and Mrs. Gold Smith, envisioned a zoo where animals could thrive in large, open enclosures that resemble their native environments.


With a small team of passionate wildlife experts, they transformed an empty piece of land into a living sanctuary for animals and a center of learning for people.


Today, Zoo Park continues their legacy by balancing recreation with education and conservation.


Each exhibit in our zoo is designed with animal comfort and environmental enrichment in mind.


Our habitats feature trees, ponds, rocks, caves, and open grasslands that mimic the wild.


We believe that animals should live as close to nature as possible, even in captivity, to ensure their physical and mental well-being.


The zoo also runs multiple conservation programs, focusing on endangered species such as the Bengal tiger, African lion, snow leopard, and Asian elephant.


Our breeding center, established in 2002, has successfully raised several rare species that are later reintroduced into protected reserves.


We collaborate with wildlife organizations, environmental NGOs, and government agencies to promote biodiversity conservation.


In addition to animal exhibits, Zoo Park features several educational and recreational facilities for visitors.


These include a Wildlife Education Center, Animal Interaction Zone, Kids’ Adventure Park, Bird Aviary, Reptile House, Butterfly Garden, and Safari Train Ride.


Our goal is not just to entertain but to educate — every visitor leaves with a better understanding of how precious and delicate our planet’s ecosystems are.


Our team includes veterinarians, zoologists, caretakers, nutritionists, and volunteers who work around the clock to ensure the animals’ health and happiness.


They monitor feeding, training, enrichment, and medical checkups every day.


The zoo hospital is equipped with modern diagnostic tools, an operating theater, and quarantine areas for new arrivals.


Zoo Park also focuses on sustainability.


We recycle waste, use solar-powered lighting in some areas, and plant over 500 new trees every year.


Our visitors are encouraged to participate in eco-friendly activities such as “Adopt an Animal” programs and weekend clean-up drives.


The zoo officially opened to the public on July 12, 1995, and welcomed over 500,000 visitors in its first year.


Today, it receives more than 1.5 million guests annually, including tourists, school groups, and nature lovers from all over the world.


We take pride in being one of the safest, cleanest, and most family-friendly zoos in the country.


One of our proudest achievements is the Wildlife Rescue & Rehabilitation Center, founded in 2010.


It provides emergency care and shelter for injured or abandoned wild animals rescued from illegal trade or deforestation zones.


Many of these animals find permanent homes in our sanctuary areas.


Our mission is to create a bridge between people and wildlife — to remind everyone that every animal, big or small, plays an important role in nature’s balance.


We believe that by connecting with animals, we can learn to respect and protect the environment we all share.


Every corner of Zoo Park tells a story — from the roaring lion enclosure to the peaceful flamingo lake.


Each visit is a new adventure filled with discovery, excitement, and learning.


We invite families, students, and nature enthusiasts to explore, experience, and be inspired.


So come, walk through our green pathways, listen to the calls of the wild, and see the wonders of nature up close.


At Zoo Park, every visit supports conservation, every moment creates awareness, and every animal has a story to tell.
